Humans: Are We An Invasive Species?

Humans (homo sapiens) originated in Africa and spread throughout Europe and Asia, eventually making their way to other continents. There are humans living on every continent, thought Antarctica has no perminent residents.

In 2014 the IUCN started the green list, a list of protected and conserved areas around the world.

In 2018 the IUCN added 15 sites to the green list, and renewed 6 already existing sites.

In 2019 the IUCN added 5 sites to the green list, and renewed 1 already existing site.

In 2020 the IUCN added 4 sites to the green list, and renewed 2 already existing sites.

In 2021 the IUCN added 10 sites to the green list, and renewed 4 already existing sites.

One issues that humans cause is deforestation, the cutting down of large areas of trees. 95% of global deforestation occurs in tropical areas, with Brazil and Indonesia accounting for almost half.

One negative impact humans have on the environment is pollution. Highlighted are some of the most polluted places in the world.

Humans burn fossil fuels which has a large impact on the environment. The burning of fossil fuels causes both air and water pollution, along with climate change.

First round of marks shows the countries with the highest oil production. The second, natural gas production. The third, coal production.

An invasive species is, by definition, an introduced organism that becomes overpopulated and harms its new environment. By this definition, we can conclude that humans are an invasive species. However, some scientists do not agree and say that humans are not an invasive species because (apart from Antarctica) there are no parts of the world where humans are non-native, and therefore cannot be invasive. Other scientists believe that humans are the invasive species, worse than any others. Some scientists believe that we are not an invasive species, just the drivers of other invasive species and the cause of most of Earth's problems. Despite scientists' disagreement on humans' identification as an invasive species, almost all can agree that humans are a problem for the well-being of the planet.
